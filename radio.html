Episodes
Episode 4

Art, Science, and the Power of Perception

April 17th, 2026

We sit down with Patricia Maurides, a visual artist and Professor of Practice in the Department of Art at Stony Brook University, whose work lives at the crossroads of science and art. With a background in biology and an MFA from Carnegie Mellon, Maurides has spent decades building courses, collaborations, and exhibitions that ask students to investigate perception, memory, and the natural world through both a scientific and artistic lens.

Episode 3

Mental Health in a Tech-Driven World

April 3rd, 2026

Exploring technology's role in graduate student mental health, featuring conversations with Donal Thomas, PhD Candidate and Graduate Student Advocate, and Kerri Mahoney, Director of Counseling and Psychological Services. The episode highlights campus resources, advocacy efforts, and strategies for maintaining wellbeing in a highly connected academic environment.

Episode 2

BRIDGES 2026 Conference Highlights, Part 2

March 20th, 2026

Continuing our BRIDGES coverage with extended interviews, featuring Alfreda S. James on networking and professional development, graduate student presenters, and musical performances from Shiqi Yang & Changyue Liu, Gina Rizzo, Kevin Kyaw, Da Young Rachel Lim, and Guilherme Batalha.

Episode 1

BRIDGES 2026 Conference Highlights, Part 1

February 20th, 2026

In the inaugural broadcast of Tech & Friends, we introduce the BRIDGES conference, a student-led initiative that brought together nearly 300 attendees. Featuring reflections from Celia Marshik, conversations with Gillian Farnan, Nancee Moes, and Peter Stokes, and highlights from award-winning work.
