Our Mission

We amplify graduate voices at the intersection of science, art, and public engagement. The magazine is a platform for interdisciplinary conversation—publishing creative, critical, and experimental work that connects research to wider communities.

Ava Nederlander

Founder, President & Co-Executive Editor

Ava is a PhD Candidate in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, working under Dr. Arie Kaufman in the Center for Visual Computing. Her research interests are interdisciplinary, focusing on the intersection of machine learning and augmented/virtual reality with medical oncologic surgeries.

Jennifer O’Connor

Vice President & Co-Executive Editor

Jennifer is a Master’s student in Neurobiology & Behavior, conducting research in the Tsirka lab. She studies immune–CNS crosstalk in inflammatory diseases such as glioblastoma, investigating how the tumor microenvironment is hijacked and modulated to facilitate growth and progression.

Veronica Borracci

Secretary

Veronica is a second-year master’s student in Geosciences working with Gregory Henkes on carbonate-clumped isotopes in shark teeth to understand thermal preferences. She also measures fossil teeth with Oliver Shipley. Previously she mapped paleogeomorphic features in northern New York. Outside the lab she’s an environmental educator who loves figure skating, hiking, and reading.

Sai Ram Kasanagottu

Treasurer

Sai Ram is a first-year PhD student in Computer Science focusing on AI, machine learning, computer vision, and natural language processing. He works on improving multimodal foundation and large language models and their real-world applications. Originally from India, he enjoys science fiction and hiking, and aims for a research career in academia or industry.

Rachel Deahl Kostelnik

Media Director

Rachel is a first-year PhD student in Marine, Atmospheric, and Sustainability Sciences (Warren Lab), researching whether marine microorganisms contribute meaningfully to ocean mixing. She holds BS/MS degrees from NJIT in Ecology & Evolution, exploring automated insect classification via infrared remote sensing. She loves writing, painting, baking, and her cats, and hopes to build a career in public-facing science communication and teaching.

Narmin Mekawy

Managing Editor

Narmin is a third-year PhD candidate in Neurobiology & Behavior (Dubnau lab) studying how stress influences the onset and progression of neurodegenerative disease. A first-generation Egyptian-Moroccan scholar from Staten Island, she organizes with community groups and loves to sing and bake. She aims to start a research lab while advancing education and science in her community.

Laiba Mahmood

Art Director

Laiba is a first-year MBA student in Marketing exploring consumer behavior and visual storytelling. As a designer, she blends art theory with marketing, believing that how knowledge is presented matters as much as the message. In her free time she bakes, reads, and learns new languages.

Esther Nosazeogie

Event Coordinator

Esther is a 3rd-year PhD student in SoMAS studying the movement ecology of great black-backed gulls in the Thorne Lab to understand their risk from offshore wind farms in the Northeast U.S. She enjoys reading, writing, and stories—especially those that reveal how exciting science and nature are.

Amanda Sirna

Communications Coordinator

Amanda is a 3rd-year PhD candidate in Mechanical Engineering studying alternative fuels—hydrogen, ammonia, and natural gas—in compression-ignition engines. She loves teaching and hopes to become a professor. In her free time she reads and paints.
